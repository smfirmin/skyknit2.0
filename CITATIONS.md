## Formal Topology of Knitting

**Every Topological Surface Can Be Knit: A Proof**
— Osinga & Gemmell, *Journal of Mathematics and the Arts*, 2009
- Proves any topological surface can be knitted; develops rubber-sheet topology model for idealized knitting
- [Journal link](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/17513470902896561)
- [ResearchGate PDF](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/232853883_Every_topological_surface_can_be_knit_A_proof)

**Adventures in Mathematical Knitting**
— Sarah-Marie Belcastro, *American Scientist*, 2013
- Accessible survey of mathematical knitting; topological surfaces, Möbius strips, Klein bottles
- [American Scientist](https://www.americanscientist.org/article/adventures-in-mathematical-knitting)

**The Topology of Knitting**
— Rebecca Gordon, University of Rochester
- Undergraduate treatment of topological methods applied to knitting
- [PDF](https://sites.math.rutgers.edu/~rlg131/topology_and_knitting.pdf)

**A Topological Study of Textile Structures. Part I: An Introduction to Topological Methods**
— Grishanov et al., *Textile Research Journal*
- Develops syntactic primitives for weft-knitted fabric structures; foundational for CAD of knitted fabrics
- [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/249785745_A_Topological_Study_of_Textile_Structures_Part_I_An_Introduction_to_Topological_Methods)

## Graph Models for Knitting

**Wooly Graphs: A Mathematical Framework for Knitting**
— Bell, 2024
- Hamiltonian/Eulerian path model for knitting graphs; formalizes stitch connectivity, held stitches, cables
- [arXiv HTML](https://arxiv.org/html/2407.00511v2) | [arXiv PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2407.00511)

**A Graph Model and a Layout Algorithm for Knitting Patterns**
— 2024
- Complexity classes of knitting graphs; planar vs non-planar; layout from pattern
- [arXiv HTML](https://arxiv.org/html/2406.13800v1)

**knit-graphs (Python package)**
- Data structure for knitted structures: yarn-edges, stitch-edges, wale-braid structure
- [PyPI](https://pypi.org/project/knit-graphs/)

## Yarn & Stitch Topology

**TopoKnit: A Process-Oriented Representation for Modeling the Topology of Yarns in Weft-Knitted Textiles**
— *Computers & Graphics*, 2021
- Contact Neighborhoods (CNs) as primitives; topology graph of yarn flow through stitches
- [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1524070321000199)

**Topological Defect Propagation to Classify Knitted Fabrics**
— 2025
- Formal knittability criteria via topological defect propagation; links manufacturability to topology
- [arXiv HTML](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.22369)

## 3D Shape & Shaping Techniques

**Stitch Meshes for Modeling Knitted Clothing with Yarn-Level Detail**
— Yuksel et al., MIT CSAIL
- Stitch mesh framework; shift-paths for row connection; short-row representation; knittable structures
- [MIT CSAIL project page](https://people.csail.mit.edu/kuiwu/stitchmodeling)

**Automated Generation of Knit Patterns for Non-Developable Surfaces**
— Popescu et al., ETH Zurich, 2017
- Warp/weft edge topology; short-row and increase/decrease as topological exceptions; course direction
- [PDF](https://block.arch.ethz.ch/brg/files/POPESCU_DMSP-2017_automated-generation-knit-patterns_1505737906.pdf)

**Computational Design of Knit Templates**
— *ACM Transactions on Graphics*, 2021
- Template-based pipeline for designing knittable garment shapes
- [ACM](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3488006)

**Unravelling the Mechanics of Knitted Fabrics Through Hierarchical Geometric Representation**
— *Proceedings of the Royal Society A*, 2024
- Yarn-based dynamical model; anisotropy from stitch topology; design space for machine-knit fabrics
- [Royal Society](https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/abs/10.1098/rspa.2023.0753) | [arXiv HTML](https://arxiv.org/html/2307.12360v3)

**Geometric Modeling of Knitted Fabrics**
— *PNAS*, 2024
- Gaussian curvature and flat-metric topology; non-flat stitch patterns for 3D shaping
- [PNAS](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2416536122)

**Unraveling the Physics of Knitting**
— Georgia Tech, 2024
- Stitch topology governs elasticity; knitting as additive manufacturing; overcrossing/undercrossing
- [Georgia Tech News](https://www.gatech.edu/news/2024/06/03/unraveling-physics-knitting)
